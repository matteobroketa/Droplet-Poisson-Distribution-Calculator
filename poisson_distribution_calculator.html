The Poisson model assumes independent events at a constant average rate. For droplet assays, λ is the expected number of cells or molecules per droplet.

Theoretical λ in droplets

Estimate λ from droplet diameter and input concentration.

Volume for a spherical droplet: V(pL) = (π / 6) × diameter³ / 1000
λ = cells/mL × V(pL) / 1,000,000,000

Observed λ from occupancy

Convert measured positive droplet occupancy into λ using the zero-class relation.

Enter a fraction from 0 to 1, or a percent from 0 to 100.
If p is the positive fraction, then P(empty) = 1 − p = e−λ
Observed λ = −ln(1 − p)

Co-encapsulation probability

Calculate independent Poisson probabilities for two populations and multiply them for a joint event.

The joint probability assumes independent encapsulation for population 1 and population 2.

How to use this calculator

This file is self-contained. Save it anywhere and open it in a browser.

  • Distribution: enter λ, choose a maximum x, and calculate the probability mass function.
  • Probability query: choose a statement such as P(X ≥ x) and enter x.
  • Droplet λ: assumes spherical droplets and converts micrometer diameter to picoliters.
  • Observed λ: enter positive droplet occupancy as either a fraction or a percent.
  • Co-encapsulation: multiplies independent Poisson probabilities.
